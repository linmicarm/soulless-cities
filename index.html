an intown atlanta field guide

NON-PLACE DETECTOR

Marc Augé named the non-place, a space built for movement and transaction rather than belonging. This is a guide to intown Atlanta ranked by the opposite: how alive a place feels. The livelier a spot, the more it looks like Atlanta. As it drains toward a non-place, the color and the collage drain with it, because a non-place could be anywhere.

The ranking

Five axes (Human Scale, Encounter, Friction, Uniqueness, Street Engagement), weighted toward belonging. Four are authored judgments; Encounter can be nudged by live OpenStreetMap data, the one signal OSM tags reliably. Map tiles by CARTO and OpenStreetMap contributors. MARTA rail data approximate, for orientation. Concept after Marc Augé and Jan Gehl. Scores are one reading, not gospel. Argue with them.
